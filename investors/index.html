Scale

Deployment & scalability

  1. Modular side-by-side scaling

    As a self-enclosed module, scalability is straightforward: units are placed side by side, and an electrical line provides power at standard outputs.

    400 VAC / 50 Hz → 6.5 kWel peak output per engine (8 engines for a 50 kWel system). 480 VAC / 60 Hz → 7.5 kWel peak per engine (7 engines for 50 kWel).

  2. Short lead time

    Lead time is low and multiple units can be delivered within a few months — power today, not in ten years.

  3. Incremental CAPEX

    Buy power as you need it: power 10% of your data center to sell compute to customers today, and defer CAPEX compared to a 10-year nuclear plant or equivalent long-lead, high-cost power infrastructure.

Units are placed outside at 85°C surface temperature with no additional cooling costs inside the data center.

Economics

Fixed OPEX outlook (20 years)

High initial CAPEX offset by elimination of electricity bills and carbon exposure — minimal OPEX relative to diesel and grid volatility.

  1. Shielded from inflation & carbon policy

    High initial CAPEX is offset by eliminating electricity bills and carbon taxes for two decades. Your data center is shielded from 28 years of inflation, carbon taxes, and 700+ fuel-truck delivery price fluctuations.

  2. 12× lower cumulative OPEX vs. diesel

    Cumulative OPEX is 12× less than a diesel generator and does not increase year on year.

  3. Zero fuel volatility

    Fuel is pre-loaded — no monthly gas bills or carbon credits. Isotope reliability means no unexpected downtime and no hidden costs.

  4. Continuous availability

    In remote or mission-critical settings, isotope power provides continuous baseload for up to 28 years, 24/7, nonstop.

  5. Minimal maintenance

    Minimal moving parts compared to diesel turbines — approximately 2 hours of maintenance per year by experienced technicians.

  6. Risk mitigation asset

    No risk of a missed fuel delivery: a complex logistical supply chain is replaced with a simple, depreciable asset.
